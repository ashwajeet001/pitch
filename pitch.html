Dejunk: Circular Fashion at Scale

Zero water. Zero chemicals. 100% upcycled.
Production Ready

Textile waste, particularly leftover threads from manufacturing, is a hidden yet significant contributor to the fashion industry's environmental crisis. This pre-production waste accumulates in staggering quantities annually—most ending up in landfills where they emit harmful gases for centuries, or are incinerated, worsening air quality. The UN estimates that a single pair of jeans requires 7,500–10,000 liters of water. One garbage truck full of clothes is burned or dumped every second. On average, 35% of all materials in the supply chain end up as waste before reaching the consumer.

Our Solution: At Dejunk, we upcycle these leftovers—commonly referred to as waste—from fashion and textile industries to create meaningful products. All our products are handwoven without the use of any chemicals, artificial softeners, or even water. Our innovative processes transform waste into beautiful, handwoven products, promoting a circular model that minimizes the fashion industry's environmental footprint while directly supporting employment generation.

❌ The Problem

India's textile industry has an environmental crisis:

  • Millions of tonnes of waste generated annually
  • Traditional recycling needs 50,000+ liters water per tonne
  • Heavy chemical processing damages ecosystems
  • 90% of textile waste ends in landfills
  • No real circular infrastructure

✅ My Solution

A closed-loop model that flips the script:

  • 100% post-production waste → new products
  • Zero water consumption (yes, actually zero)
  • No chemicals, no artificial softeners
  • 60% less energy vs traditional methods
  • Reverse logistics with 15+ partners
